About myself I’ll say this:

I was born and raised in Portland, OR, became an adult in New York City, have spent long stretches in South Africa and France, and currently live on a farm outside of Nashville, Tennessee.

I got my first "job job" at the age of 19, working in a neurobehavioral research lab, studying postpartum depression using FMRI technology, and haven't had a boring job since. The line-up includes academic tutoring for high-achieving students, teaching English (in French) in France, working in book publishing as an editor, production manager, and designer, in advertising as a project manager (okay, that job was a little boring), and at a tech start-up as a product manager.

As you can imagine, going back to school to study software development was the logical next step. (I mean, who wants to stop learning, when there is SO MUCH TO LEARN!) But seriously, when you realize your two great loves are learning new things and building things, you start wondering how you didn't get into programming long, long ago. (My oft dissatisfied analytical / creative brain thanks me for taking the leap of faith.)

And to wrap up this 'About' with something of a mission-statement-y send off, I relay to you, with the upmost sincerity, that as a developer, I'd like to create software that addresses the changing economy, including how people earn money, take care of themselves and their health, and live lives of their own choosing. And I'd like to design programs that solve problems and are as intuitive as they are functional. Of course all of that's easier said than done, but I'm sure going to try.
